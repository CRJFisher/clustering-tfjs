The silent corruption
fit() starts by calling dispose() to wipe the previous fit. dispose() nulls labels_ and the affinity matrices but forgets medoid_indices_. After fit(data1) → compute_medoids() → fit(data2), the medoid indices still point into data1's rows and are presented as valid for data2. No error, no warning — wrong answer.
